Welcome.

I am a PhD student in Political Science and International Relations at the School of Graduate Studies at Ibn Haldun University and Research Fellow at Heydar Aliyev Center for Eurasian Studies.

Before joining Ibn Haldun University, I was a Climate Change Researcher at IMPACT Initiatives where I supervised the Community-based monitoring (CBM) of the STREAM programme.

I obtained a BS in International Relations from the Kyrgyz-Turkish Manas University (2022) and an MA in Politics and Security from OSCE Academy in Bishkek (2024).

I am broadly interested in the development of research methods, particularly quantitative methods, for International Political Economy data. My past research has focused on experimental designs and survey methodology, with empirical applications in climate change and natural resource management. Currently, my research interest is focused on statistical methods for causal inference with applications in International Political Economy; Advanced technology governance, Semiconductor supply chain and regional integration in Eurasian space.
