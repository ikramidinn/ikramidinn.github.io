Applied research with IMPACT Inititiatives:

1. Community-based monitoring of the STREAM Programme project

I have supervised the CBM project for the STREAM Programme, aimed to hand over ongoing monitoring capacity to beneficiaries in communities of intervention to improve project accountability, and identify additional areas where support is needed and highlight emergent needs. Thus, the purpose was to set up mutual accountability between communities and programme implementors in the selected communities of Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan and Uzbekistan.

To learn more regarding the project materials, click here.

2. Area-Based Risk Assessment of the Batken and Sughd regions of Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan

This research aimed to strengthen Disaster Risk Management in Batken (Kyrgyzstan) and Sughd (Tajikistan) by enhancing local, district, and national stakeholders’ understanding of multi-hazard risks, supporting DRCU in Kyrgyzstan, REACT in Tajikistan, and other partners to better prioritize and plan risk reduction and community preparedness through comprehensive analysis of vulnerabilities, hazard exposure, and critical infrastructure risks.
Within the assessment, I supervised the quantitative part of the research in Kyrgyzstan: development and design of questionnaire, data collection, cleaning and analysis. Was responsible for the data collection of the Batken district of Batken region, Kyrgyzstan. Conducted Key Informant Interviews, Focus Group Fiscussions (FGD) and Mapping Focus Group Discussions.

To learn more regarding the project materials, click here.

3. Area-Based Risk Assessment of Isfana Watershed in the Fergana Valley

The objective was to conduct an in-depth study of climate change impacts and human-induced pressures on the conditions of natural resources, including irrigation water and agricultural, pastoral practices. The research also considered techniques aimed at fostering the resilience of the watershed's community amidst the challenges posed by climate change, disaster risk reduction and cross-cutting issue across considered sectors.
Within the assessment, I supervised the quantitative part of the research: development and design of questionnaire, data collection, cleaning and analysis.
