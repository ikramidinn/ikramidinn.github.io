Unsuccessful attempt to teach at Manas University***

***In July 2024, I was awarded the OSCE Academy Junior Teaching Fellowship grant to teach at one of the local universities. I had planned to teach a course on International Political Economy, which was initially approved by the Department of International Relations. The course was listed under ULS-312; however, at the beginning of the Fall semester, it was blocked by the University Rectorate. The reason given was that, since I did not hold a PhD degree, I was not eligible to teach at the university. This experience became one of the key motivations for me to pursue PhD studies.

ULS-312: International Political Economy, Fall 2024

Course Description:
This course provides an introduction to the field of International Political Economy (IPE). It examines the interplay between politics and economics on a global scale, exploring how political institutions, processes, and actors influence economic policies and outcomes, and vice versa. Key topics include trade, finance, development, and globalization.

Learning Objectives:
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
1. Understand the key concepts and theories in IPE.
2. Analyze the impact of international political and economic policies.
3. Evaluate the roles of various international institutions.
4. Discuss contemporary issues in global trade, finance, and development.
5. Develop critical thinking and analytical skills in the context of IPE.
